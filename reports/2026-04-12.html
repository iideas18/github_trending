VoxCPM2: Tokenizer-Free TTS for Multilingual Speech Generation, Creative Voice Design, and True-to-Life Cloning

VoxCPM is a state-of-the-art tokenizer-free Text-to-Speech system that generates continuous speech representations through an end-to-end diffusion autoregressive architecture. Unlike traditional TTS models that rely on discrete audio tokenization, VoxCPM operates entirely in a continuous latent space, enabling highly natural and expressive speech synthesis. The latest release, VoxCPM2, is a 2-billion parameter model trained on over 2 million hours of multilingual speech data, supporting 30 languages with 48kHz studio-quality audio output.

Key features include Voice Design (creating new voices from natural-language descriptions alone), Controllable Voice Cloning (cloning any voice from a short reference clip with style guidance), and Ultimate Cloning (faithfully reproducing every vocal nuance). The model achieves a real-time factor as low as ~0.13 on an NVIDIA RTX 4090 with the Nano-vLLM acceleration engine, and requires only ~8 GB of VRAM. It's fully open-source under the Apache-2.0 license, making it commercially viable.

VoxCPM2 is trending as one of the most capable open-source TTS systems available, outperforming many closed-source alternatives on standard benchmarks. It's particularly relevant for developers building voice-enabled applications, content creators needing multilingual narration, and researchers exploring the frontiers of speech synthesis. Built on the MiniCPM-4 backbone from OpenBMB, it represents a significant step forward in making high-quality, controllable speech generation accessible to everyone.

所有小初高、大学PDF教材。

ChinaTextbook is an open-source repository that collects and organizes PDF textbooks from China's education system, covering elementary school, middle school, high school, and university levels. The project primarily focuses on mathematics textbooks from the People's Education Press (人教版), including subjects like calculus, linear algebra, discrete mathematics, and probability theory at the university level. It was created to combat the practice of reselling freely available educational resources with private watermarks.

The repository addresses a practical challenge with GitHub's file size limits by including a file-merging tool for PDFs that exceed the 50MB upload threshold. Files larger than 50MB are split into 35MB chunks that can be recombined using the provided merger utility. The project also maintains a Telegram community for updates and collaboration.

This project is trending due to its mission of promoting educational equity and open access to learning materials. It serves a particularly important purpose for overseas Chinese families who want their children to continue learning with Chinese educational curricula, as well as anyone interested in exploring China's K-12 and university-level mathematics education. The project represents a grassroots effort to democratize access to educational content.
