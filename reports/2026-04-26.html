DeepEP: an efficient expert-parallel communication library

DeepEP is an efficient expert-parallel communication library released by DeepSeek AI, purpose-built for Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) training and inference. It provides high-throughput, low-latency all-to-all GPU kernels that handle MoE dispatch and combine operations — the communication-heavy steps that typically bottleneck large-model training.

The library implements asymmetric-domain bandwidth forwarding (e.g., NVLink ↔ RDMA), pure-RDMA low-latency kernels for inference decoding, FP8 dispatch with BF16 combine, SM-count control, and a hook-based communication-computation overlap mechanism that frees up streaming multiprocessors. Benchmarks on H800 GPUs with CX7 InfiniBand show NVLink throughput close to hardware limits and inference dispatch latencies under 200 µs even at 256-way expert parallelism.

It’s trending again following a recent 30% performance contribution from Tencent and a NVLink-leveraging update to the low-latency path. DeepEP is essential infrastructure for anyone training or serving MoE models at the scale of DeepSeek-V3/R1 and is becoming the de facto open-source reference for production-grade EP communication.

Specification and documentation for the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)

The Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) is an open-standard specification that defines a common language and functional primitives for interoperable commerce. It allows AI agents, apps, payment service providers, and credential providers to discover business capabilities, negotiate checkout sessions, and exchange payment tokens through a uniform protocol — eliminating one-off integrations between every storefront and platform.

UCP is built around composable Capabilities (Checkout, Identity Linking, Order webhooks, Payment Token Exchange) and pluggable Extensions (discounts, fulfillment, etc.). It is transport-agnostic — businesses can expose Capabilities over REST, MCP (Model Context Protocol), or A2A — and supports advanced security primitives like AP2 mandates and verifiable credentials, making it especially well-suited to autonomous AI shopping agents.

It’s trending because agentic commerce is becoming a hot frontier in 2026: as LLM-driven assistants begin to fill carts and complete checkouts on users' behalf, the industry needs a shared protocol — and UCP is positioning itself as that standard, alongside emerging efforts like Google's AP2.
